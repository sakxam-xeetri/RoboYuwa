ORGANIZATION OVERVIEW
- Name: RoboYuwa
- Nature: Youth-driven NGO focused on robotics, technology, research, and innovation.
- Core purpose: Build an innovative mindset among youth, provide technical platforms, and strengthen leadership/confidence in robotics and technical research.
- Mission: Empower youth with technology skills, creative problem-solving, and leadership to transform communities.
- Working values found across documents: transparency, accountability, equality, inclusiveness, ethical technology use, and community impact.

KEY OBJECTIVES (Constitution + Orientation)
- Promote hands-on learning, innovation, and creativity in robotics and technology.
- Develop leadership, teamwork, and problem-solving through projects/workshops/community programs.
- Unite young minds for science and technology collaboration.
- Encourage ethical and sustainable use of technology.
- Build robotics/technology projects and participate in competitions.
- Organize robotics/technology events and sub-events.

JOURNEY & MILESTONES
- 2024 June: Foundation of RoboYuwa.
- 2024 Aug: First international competition participation (Technoxian World Robotics Championship).
- 2024 Nov: Program expansion to 3 new districts; 500+ youth participants mentioned.
- 2025 May: Aavishkar competition - secured second prize.
- 2026 Jan: RADAR26 annual planning/event milestone.

ACHIEVEMENTS & IMPACT SNAPSHOT
- Participation in national-level robotics competitions.
- Representation of Nepal in Technoxian World Robotics Championship.
- School-level robotics workshops organized.
- 40+ structured workshops/bootcamps (as stated in presentation).
- 1,200+ youth reached (presentation impact figure).
- Multiple regional/national awards; partnership development with schools and organizations.

MAJOR INITIATIVES/PROJECTS MENTIONED
- RoboLearn Initiative: 12-week robotics curriculum for underserved schools.
- InnovatYouth Summit: annual youth solution showcase judged by experts.
- Tech4Community: youth-led low-cost tech solutions for local organizations.

GOVERNANCE STRUCTURE
- Executive/Board roles repeatedly listed:
  President, Vice-President, Secretary, Joint Secretary, Treasurer,
  Technical & Research Head, Outreach Head, Creative Head,
  Event Planner/Management Head, Legalities Head, Advisors, Proxy Head.
- Tenure generally stated as 2 years.
- Governance through General Assembly, Executive Committee, and Advisory Committee/Board.

ROLE CLARITY (from orientation)
- President: vision, coordination, external representation, major planning.
- Vice-President: internal representation, execution follow-up, acts for president when absent.
- Secretary/Joint Secretary: administration, minutes, reports, official communications.
- Treasurer: financial approvals/audits/transparency and expenditure tracking.
- Technical Head: technical decisions, research direction, workshop/project support.
- Outreach Head: sponsorships/partnerships/external relations.
- Creative Head: branding/social media/content.
- Event Head: event planning, logistics, execution.
- Legalities Head: constitutional/legal compliance and legal documents.
- Advisors: strategic guidance.

MEMBERSHIP FRAMEWORK (Constitution)
- Categories: Tenure-based, Honorary, Volunteers, Advisors.
- Voting: tenure-based and eligible honorary members generally one vote each; volunteers/advisors non-voting unless specified.
- Membership fee clauses found:
  - NPR 800 for 2-year tenure membership.
  - NPR 200 renewal fee.
  - Advisory/volunteers/honorary exempted from fee.
- Membership termination/renewal/reinstatement and due-process clauses are included.

FINANCE, APPROVALS, AND COMPLIANCE
- Funding sources: sponsorships, donations, membership fees, events.
- Expenditure approval: multiple clauses indicate 51% board agreement for approvals.
- Project funding clause: up to NPR 5,000 can be approved by designated leadership set; beyond that requires board-level approval (as per constitution OCR text).
- Treasurer responsible for records, reporting, annual budget, and audit flow.
- Legal compliance references: Association Registration Act (2034) and Social Welfare Council Act (2049).

MEETINGS, ELECTIONS, AND INTERNAL OPERATIONS
- Monthly general member meetings, weekly departmental meetings, and emergency meeting provisions are described.
- Election Sub-Committee based election model, nomination process, candidacy eligibility, secret ballot provisions, and dispute handling are included.
- No-confidence motion and vacancy-filling provisions exist.
- Code of conduct, reporting discipline, and interdepartmental coordination expectations are documented.
